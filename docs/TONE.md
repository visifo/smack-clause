# SmackClause Branding & Voice

## The Mission
Guard clauses are often dry and polite. SmackClause is not. Our goal is to make validation feel like a protective barrier—strong, industrial, and uncompromising.

## Tone of Voice
- **Direct**: Use strong verbs. "Smack," "Hit," "Stop," "Violate."
- **Technical**: Errors are "Violations." Input is "Payload." Logic is the "Happy Path."
- **Aggressive but Precise**: We aren't mean; we're accurate. We don't say "Please provide an integer." We say "Expected int, got string."

## Visual Direction
- **Palette**: Charcoal Base (`#1A1A1A`), Warning Red (`#FF3E3E`), and Muted Amber (`#FFB800`).
- **Typography**: Monospace for everything technical. Bold, condensed slabs for headers.
- **Iconography**: Boxing bells, impact bursts, and industrial hazard stripes.

## Error Message Guidelines
Errors should be structured as "The Crime Scene."
1. **The Violation**: What rule was broken.
2. **The Culprit**: The path/variable name.
3. **The Evidence**: The actual value received.
4. **The Location**: Exactly where the smack happened in the code.
